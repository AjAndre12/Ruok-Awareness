About RUOK?

RUOK? is a public health promotion charity focused on encouraging everyone to meaningfully connect with people around them and support anyone struggling with life. The RUOK? mission is to inspire and empower everyone to meaningfully connect with people around them and support anyone struggling with life by asking, "Are you OK?"

The RUOK? movement was founded by Gavin Larkin in 2009, following the suicide of his father. Since then, the movement has grown nationwide, with an annual RUOK? Day held every September to raise awareness about mental health and the importance of checking in on each other.

Whether you're concerned about a loved one, a friend, or a colleague, RUOK? provides the tools and resources needed to have meaningful conversations that can change lives. Don't wait for a sign—take action today.

How to Ask

Starting the conversation can be daunting, but it’s crucial. Follow these four simple steps:

1. Ask R U OK?

Be relaxed, friendly, and concerned in your approach. Don't hesitate to ask, "Are you OK?"

2. Listen

Listen with an open mind and show them you’re listening by nodding or saying things like "I see" or "I understand."

3. Encourage Action

Encourage them to take action, whether it's talking to a trusted person, seeing a doctor, or contacting a helpline.

4. Check In

Make sure to check in later to see how they’re doing. Your ongoing support can make a big difference.
